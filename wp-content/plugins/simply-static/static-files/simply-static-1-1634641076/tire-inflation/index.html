Tire Inflation

Having a proper inflation ensures maximum performance and a long life from your tires, but most importantly it ensures your safety as well. By checking the air pressure once a month, you will be able to see it the tires are under-or-over- inflated which can result in accidents.

Here are some useful steps to measure the pressure of the tires.

1) Find the tire pressure level required for your car. This information is usually on a yellow sticker in the doorjamb on the driver side. It is also contained in the owner’s manual. The pressure level may differ for front and rear tires.

2) Adjust the air pressure when the tires are cold.

3)when removing the cap, set it aside that way you do not lose it. It is quite important that you put the cap back to avoid moisture entering the tire.

4) Measure the pressure and compare it with the PSI required by the owner’s manual.

Frequently Asked Questions

You might have questions, we have answers.

What does installation include?

When you buy tires, installation includes our life of tire maintenance, mounting, balancing and valve stems (or a TPMS rebuild kit).

How long will the work take once you arrive on-site?

This depends upon the work required and the vehicle we are working on. It does not take any longer than having the work done in a store. The difference is you don’t have to wait in line, service is personal “one-on-one” and you can do whatever you need to do, while we do the work.

How long do the tires take to deliver?

In emergency cases, tires can be sourced as early as same day. But most non-emergency cases can be sourced and installed within 24-48 hours

How can I be sure I’m ordering the correct tire size?

By shopping on Restires.com and entering your vehicle information, you will find the accurate tire sizing information for your exact vehicle. We also recommend doing a quick physical check of the tire size markings on your current tires to ensure a 100% exact fit.

What brands do you offer?

We offer all major brands at all levels and price points. From Premium brands like Bridgestone to budget brands like Advanta.

Do you attend roadside breakdowns?

Most of our work is pre-booked, although we will always try to attend any situations where circumstances allow.

Do you sell used tires?

No, due to safety concerns with unknown used tire history, we have a strict “NO USED TIRE” policy

Do the tires come with any warranty?

All tires sold come with their default tire manufacturers warranty against manufacturing defects. Some manufacturers also provide a built in Road Hazard Warranty. Please see individual manufacturer information for specific details and also we offer road hazard warranty with the purchase of any tire, as well as many other perk  including discounts, free shipping, flat battery jumps, and more
