Are your tires ready for 2021?

It’s common knowledge that even under the best road conditions driving on worn-out tires is unsafe. But how safe is driving with worn-out tires?

It’s common knowledge that even under the best road conditions driving on worn-out tires is unsafe. But how safe is driving with worn-out tires? The National Center for Statistics and Analysis at the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration recently conducted a study that found 26.2% of auto crashes involved vehicles with under condition tires. Each car and its respective driving are different, but following this study, we can conclude that drivers that do not take tire care seriously have a greater chance of suffering a car accident.

How can we be sure that our tires are in safe condition?

The easiest way to ensure that your tires are in good condition is the penny test. Place a penny headfirst into several tread grooves across the tire. If you always see the top of Lincoln’s head, your treads are shallow and worn. If this is the case, your tires need to be replaced. If the tread always covers part of Lincoln’s head, you have more than 2/32 of an inch of tread depth remaining. It means you probably don’t need new tires.

Certain signs can also indicate to the driver that the tires may be worn and need to be replaced.

When driving, if the vehicle seems to be misaligned and the car seems to fall to one side, it is a sign associated with tire wear. If the steering wheel has an unusual vibration, This vibration can also be explained by the wear the tires may have or the incorrect pressure they have; in this case, the steering wheel will also seem to feel every obstacle the tires encounter on the road. If the braking distance time is longer than usual is also a sign of tire wear, the tread grip is not in good condition, leading to a longer braking distance. Tires that show uneven wear are also a risk indication. It’s also imperative to check regularly the tires for scratches, cuts, or protrusions on the rims or tires; this can lead to tire wear and even bursting.

How can we make our tires last longer?

Knowing the correct tire pressure will not only ensure greater safety when riding on the road but also ensure that your car’s tire tread has a uniform, longer wear and tear, helping you save some money in the long run.

Using low-pressure tires will make more tire surface in contact with the road, increasing the tire wear and fuel consumption. When you use a higher pressure than recommended for your type of car, this will cause all the weight to be supported by the tire’s central thread, thus preventing it from molding so easily to the dynamics of the road, speeding up the tread wear process, especially when running at higher speeds, and may run the risk of bursting.
As we have seen, knowing the correct tire pressure will help you keep these components in good condition for longer and ensure that they always circulate in maximum safety conditions. The right tire pressure varies from car to car since not all cars have the same weight, but it will always vary between 2.2 BAR and 2.5 BAR.

The correct tire pressure values for each car can be found on a metal plate on the driver’s door of your car and should be consulted if you want more detailed information on the ideal pressure advised by the manufacturer.

Tire alignment, also known as wheel alignment, can help your tires work correctly and help them last longer. You can also improve handling and prevent your vehicle from moving in one direction or vibrating strangely on the road.
Tire rotation. The front tires of the vehicle often wear out faster than the rear ones. Changing their position frequently helps to achieve more even wear and allows maximum tread life.
It is recommended to rotate the tires approximately every 7,000 Miles. Other factors may mean that it is necessary to rotate the tires more frequently. If you regularly drive at high speed and over long distances or carry heavy loads, the additional stress may imply slightly more frequent rotations.
If they emit a buzz while driving on a regular road, it may be time to check if rotation is necessary. It is only possible to rotate the tires from front to rear if they are all the same size. It is always necessary to balance the wheels when rotating tires.
